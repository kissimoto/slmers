Eliminating Paper Trails in UK Motorcycle Training

How Kiwacht-Edge saves ATBs from voided DVSA certificates, manual errors, and wasted hours

The Pain Point

UK motorcycle training schools (ATBs) face a paper-based workflow when issuing certificates:

  • DVSA sells books of 25 paper certificates to Approved Training Bodies (ATBs)
  • After each successful CBT, the instructor fills out the certificate by hand
  • Staff then manually retype all 25 certs into Excel for DVSA submission
  • One typo = voided certificate = £8 loss
  • For a small school: 3 voids per 20 clients and 12 hours of retyping work = £1200/year lost

Some of the Quirky DVSA Rules

Duration must be entered in decimal hours — but not how you’d expect:

  • 1 hour = 1.00
  • 15 minutes = 0.25
  • 30 minutes = 0.50 and so on

Get this wrong? Certificate voided.

The Kiwacht-Edge Workflow

  1. Instructor or admin opens Kiwacht-Edge PWA on any smartphone (iOS/Android)
  2. Enters the required DVSA fields such as:
    • ATB Number
    • Certificate Number (auto-incremented: 1–25)
    • Completion Time
    • Course Duration (e.g., “4h 12m”)
    • Driver Number (9-char DVLA ID)
    • Instructor Certificate and more
  3. Kiwacht-Edge applies DVSA rules:
    • Converts “4h 12m” → 4.25
    • Validates driver number format
    • Prevents duplicate cert numbers
  4. Data sent securely to training school’s on-prem hub (a €500 PC in the office)
  5. Hub collates all 25 certs, generates DVSA-compliant CSV
  6. Owner reviews → approves → exports
  7. All data that is sent to the DVSA is erased - amnesia by design

Why This Matters for the UK

This isn’t just about motorcycles. It’s about finding niche solutions for 99% of European businesses:

  • ✅ Niche but real: ~500 ATBs, ~2,000 instructors in the UK alone
  • ✅ Regulated & high-stakes: DVSA compliance is non-negotiable
  • ✅ Sovereign by necessity: No cloud AI can handle paper-to-digital conversion with zero data leakage

Outcome: 12 hours saved/month plus £1,200/year recovered. Zero voids.
