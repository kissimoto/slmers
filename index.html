What is an SLM?

Small Language Models (SLMs) sit at the opposite end of the spectrum from Large Language Models (LLMs).

LLMs are designed to extract knowledge from vast datasets, for which they require a lot of investment in infrastructure to power these propositions.

SLMs on the other hand are green, efficient and in the case of our solution KiWacht and KiWacht-Edge incorporate human input through user defined rules.

Why Gmail’s AI Is a Watershed Moment

Google’s rollout of AI in Gmail isn’t just a feature update — it’s a strategic shift: your most sensitive business communications are now processed by default on US servers.

Even if Google claims it “doesn’t train on your data,” the act of sending every email to its cloud creates three irreversible risks:

  1. Invisible data flows: Under US Executive Order 14086, US authorities can access data held by US companies — including EU citizens’ emails — with limited redress.
  2. Passive extraction: “Free” AI is paid for with your workflow intelligence — client briefs, contract terms, creative IP — feeding market insights and behavioral models.
  3. Sovereignty as opt-out: The default path leads to dependency. Choosing privacy now requires deliberate effort.

For European SMEs, this undermines GDPR, erodes client trust, and weakens strategic autonomy.

Kiwacht-Edge offers a different path: AI that stays in your office, obeys your rules, and never phones home. Because sovereignty isn’t a feature. It’s the foundation.
