What is an SLM?

Small Language Models (SLMs) sit at the opposite end of the spectrum from Large Language Models (LLMs).

LLMs are designed to extract knowledge from vast datasets, often without the explicit knowledge or consent of the users whose data they ingest.

LLMs require vast investments in infrastructure to power these propositions.

SLMs are the exact opposite, they are green, efficient and in the case of KiWacht incorporate human input through user defined rules.

Why SLMs?

Europe must reduce dependency on US and Chinese cloud AI. SLMs offer a sovereign path - no data export, no vendor lock-in.

AI Doesn't Need Reactors

While Europe invests in Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) to power energy-intensive AI, Kiwacht proves sovereign intelligence can be lightweight, local, and sustainable.

Why SLMs?

Large Language Models rely on infrastructure controlled by US and Chinese tech giants, creating strategic vulnerability for European organisations.

Small Language Models (SLMs) are compact, efficient, and locally deployable and enable true digital sovereignty. They run on European hardware, under European control, compliant with GDPR from day one.

This isn't about rejecting innovation. It's about reclaiming agency in the AI era.
